# Paid Ad Budget Planner — Maine Mortgage Lead Gen

Realistic cost ranges and a recommended phased budget. These are industry
ballpark figures (mortgage is a competitive, high-CPC vertical) — actual
costs vary by season, targeting, and creative quality. Track actuals in
`ad-spend-tracker.csv` and adjust monthly.

---

## Platform Cost Ranges (Mortgage/Financial Services Vertical)

| Platform | Typical Cost | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Meta (Facebook/Instagram) Lead Ads** | $20-60 CPL (cost per lead) | Lower cost, but leads are often early-stage ("just looking") — needs strong nurture sequence |
| **Google Local Services Ads (LSA)** | $20-80 per lead, pay-per-lead model | Higher intent (people actively searching "mortgage broker near me"), Google verifies your license/background |
| **Google Search Ads (PPC)** | $10-25 CPC, mortgage keywords are expensive | Pay per click, not per lead — conversion rate matters a lot |
| **LinkedIn Ads** | $50-100+ CPL | Best for B2B (reaching Realtors/CPAs directly), not consumer leads |

## Recommended Phased Approach

### Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Organic + Relationship Focus, $0-200/mo
- Focus entirely on referral partner outreach (free) and organic content
  (free, just time investment)
- Optional: $5-10/day ($150-300/mo) Meta ads boosting your best-performing
  organic posts (not full lead-ad campaigns yet) — builds local audience
  and retargeting pool cheaply

### Phase 2 (Months 4-6): Test Paid Lead Gen, $300-600/mo
- **Google Local Services Ads**: Start with a modest weekly budget cap
  (~$50-75/week = ~$250-300/mo). LSA is pay-per-lead (not per click), so
  cost is more predictable. High intent = better ROI starting point.
- **Meta Lead Ads**: Run 1-2 campaigns at $10/day ($300/mo) targeting
  specific audiences (e.g., first-time buyers age 25-40 in target counties,
  or veterans for VA loan content).
- Track cost-per-lead AND cost-per-funded-loan for each channel — this is
  the number that actually matters.

### Phase 3 (Months 6+): Scale What Works, $600-1,500+/mo
- Double down on whichever channel has the best cost-per-funded-loan
- Consider retargeting campaigns (Meta) for people who visited
  baxtermortgage.com but didn't convert
- Add LinkedIn ads if referral-partner B2B growth is a priority

## Break-Even Math (Know Your Numbers)
Before spending on ads, know:
1. **Average commission/revenue per funded loan** (ask your branch
   manager/comp plan)
2. **Lead → Application conversion rate** (industry rough avg: 10-20% for
   paid leads, higher for referrals)
3. **Application → Funded conversion rate** (varies widely, 40-70% typical
   for qualified leads)

**Example math:**
- $300/mo on Meta Lead Ads at $40 CPL = ~7-8 leads/month
- 15% convert to application = ~1 application/month
- 50% of applications fund = 1 funded loan every 2 months
- If your revenue per loan covers more than ~$600 in ad spend, it's
  profitable — but **this assumes good follow-up**. A lead that doesn't get
  called within an hour (see `client-pipeline/follow-up-sequences.md`) is
  wasted spend.

**Bottom line:** Paid ads amplify a working follow-up system — they don't
fix a broken one. Get your referral pipeline and follow-up sequences
running first (free), then layer in paid spend once you can handle the
volume.
