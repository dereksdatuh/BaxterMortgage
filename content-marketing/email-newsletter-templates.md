# Email Newsletter Templates

Send monthly to: past clients, referral partners, and warm leads. Keep it
short, useful, and personal — this is a relationship tool, not a sales
blast.

---

## Monthly "Maine Market & Money" Newsletter Template

**Subject line options:**
- "Your [Month] Maine market update + a quick favor"
- "What's happening in Maine real estate this month"
- "[Month] check-in from Derek"

**Structure:**

```
Hi [First Name],

Hope you're doing well! Here's your [Month] update — quick and useful, I
promise.

📊 MAINE MARKET SNAPSHOT
[1-2 sentences on what's happening locally — inventory, rates trend
direction (general, not specific quotes), buyer/seller conditions]

💡 THIS MONTH'S TIP
[One practical tip — e.g., "If your homeowners insurance renewed this
year, it might be worth a quick rate-shop — happy to connect you with a
trusted local agent."]

🏡 SPOTLIGHT
[Rotate: a closed client story (with permission), a referral partner
shoutout, or a loan program spotlight]

🤝 QUICK FAVOR
Know anyone thinking about buying, selling, or refinancing in the next
few months? I'd love an introduction — I'll take great care of them, same
as I did for you.

As always, if your situation has changed (new job, paid off debt, thinking
about a second home, rates moving), it's worth a 10-minute check-in. No
pressure, just making sure you're in the best position possible.

Talk soon,
Derek
```

## "Just Closed" Client Drip Sequence

### Day 1 (Closing Day)
**Subject:** Congratulations on your new home! 🎉
> Hi [First Name], congratulations — you're officially a homeowner! It was
> a pleasure working with you. If you need anything at all (insurance
> referrals, contractor recommendations, questions about your mortgage
> statement), just reach out.

### Day 30
**Subject:** How's the new place?
> Hi [First Name], hope you're settling in well! A couple quick things:
> - Your first mortgage payment is due around [date] — let me know if you
>   have questions about your statement.
> - If you need any local recommendations (plumber, landscaper, etc.), I
>   know a lot of great people around Maine — happy to connect you.

### Month 6
**Subject:** Quick 6-month check-in
> Hi [First Name], can't believe it's been 6 months already! Just checking
> in — everything going smoothly with the home and the mortgage? If
> anything has changed (income, plans for renovations, thinking about a
> HELOC for projects), happy to chat anytime.
>
> Also — if you've been happy with how things went, a quick Google review
> would mean a lot (link: [your Google review link]). And if you know
> anyone else getting ready to buy, I'd love an introduction!

### Annual "Mortgage Check-Up"
**Subject:** Your annual mortgage check-up
> Hi [First Name], it's been a year since closing — time flies! I like to
> reach out annually to make sure your mortgage still fits your life:
> - Rates/market conditions may have changed — worth checking if a refi
>   makes sense
> - If your home's value has gone up, a HELOC might be a useful tool for
>   upcoming projects
> - If anything major changed (income, family size, plans to move), let's
>   talk through your options
>
> No pressure — just want to make sure you're getting the most out of your
> homeownership. Reply or call anytime: (207) 468-6998

## New Lead Nurture Sequence (for people who inquired but aren't ready yet)

### Immediately
**Subject:** Thanks for reaching out!
> Hi [First Name], thanks for getting in touch! I know buying a home (or
> refinancing) can feel overwhelming — my goal is to make this as
> stress-free as possible. When's a good time for a quick 15-minute call
> so I can answer your questions and give you a clear picture of your
> options?

### 1 Week Later (if no response)
**Subject:** No rush — here when you're ready
> Hi [First Name], just wanted to follow up — totally understand if now
> isn't the right time. When you're ready (whether that's next week or next
> year), I'm here. In the meantime, here's a quick guide on [first-time
> homebuyer programs in Maine / refinancing basics] that might help: [link
> if available].

### Monthly (ongoing, low-pressure)
> Add to your newsletter list — they'll get the monthly "Maine Market &
> Money" email automatically, keeping you top of mind without manual
> follow-up.
