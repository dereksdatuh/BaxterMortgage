### Script R1: "Why I became a broker, not a banker" (30s, personal)
> I could have gone to work for a bank. And honestly, I thought about it.
> But here's the thing with banks — your job is to sell that bank's product. One set of rates. One set of rules.
> I wanted to be on the buyer's side. So I became a broker.
> I have access to 20 wholesale lenders. My job is to shop them and find what actually fits you — not what fits the bank.
> I'm Derek. I'm in Kennebunk. If you're buying or refinancing in Southern Maine, reach out.
> That's what I'm here for.

### Script R2: "The rate isn't the only number" (45s, education)
> Everyone asks me about the rate. And yes, the rate matters. But it's one number.
> Here's what else matters: the loan program, the term, the points, the fees, the closing cost structure, and how long you plan to stay in the home.
> A lower rate with two points upfront might cost you more than a slightly higher rate with no points — depending on your break-even timeline.
> This is exactly what I walk every client through. It's free. It takes 20 minutes. And it's probably worth it.
> DM me or call. Let's run the real numbers.

### Script R3: "What actually happens when you apply" (45s, process)
> A lot of people are scared to apply for a mortgage because they don't know what's going to happen.
> So here's exactly what happens.
> You fill out an application. Takes about 20-30 minutes. I'll send you a list of documents — think pay stubs, tax returns, bank statements.
> I pull credit — one inquiry, minimal impact.
> Then I shop your file across lenders and come back with options. We pick one, lock the rate, go to underwriting.
> Underwriting is going to want more documents. This is normal. Don't panic when I call.
> Clear to close — this is the good email. Then closing day. You sign, you get keys.
> Start to finish, 30-45 days typically.
> Any questions? DM me.

### Script R4: "What a pre-approval letter actually does" (30s)
> When you make an offer on a home in Maine and you don't have a pre-approval letter — the listing agent knows.
> And in a competitive market, that offer goes to the bottom of the pile. Or gets ignored entirely.
> A pre-approval letter says: I've been vetted. I'm real. My financing is ready.
> It costs you nothing. It takes 48 hours. And it's the difference between getting the house and watching someone else get it.
> DM me. Let's get yours done.

### Script R5: "First-time buyer in Maine — start here" (60s, flagship)
> If you're a first-time buyer in Southern Maine and you don't know where to start, this one's for you.
> Step one: figure out your budget. Not what Zillow says you can afford. What a lender actually qualifies you for. These are different.
> Step two: get pre-approved. Do this before you tour a single home. Do not fall in love with a house you can't write an offer on.
> Step three: understand your down payment options. Depending on where you're buying and your income, you might qualify for USDA at zero down, or a down payment assistance program that stacks with FHA.
> Step four: find your team. A local lender — me — and a local agent who knows the market.
> Step five: be patient. This market moves fast but the right home exists. We'll find it.
