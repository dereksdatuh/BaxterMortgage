# Loan Program Cheat Sheet — Baxter Mortgage Maine

Quick-reference talking points for each program Baxter Mortgage offers.
Use these in conversations with referral partners (so they know who to send
you) and with clients (to quickly match them to the right program).

---

## Purchase Programs

### Conventional
- **Best for:** Borrowers with solid credit (typically 620+) and steady
  income/assets.
- **Down payment:** As low as 3-5%.
- **Pitch to Realtors:** "Strong file, clean appraisal contingencies, fast
  close — great for competitive offers."

### FHA
- **Best for:** First-time buyers, lower credit scores (down to ~580),
  higher debt-to-income ratios.
- **Down payment:** 3.5%.
- **Pitch to Realtors:** "Don't write off FHA buyers — many sellers don't
  realize how strong these offers can be when underwritten right."

### VA
- **Best for:** Veterans, active duty, National Guard/Reserve, eligible
  surviving spouses. Maine has a large veteran population.
- **Down payment:** 0%.
- **Pitch to Realtors:** "Zero down, no PMI, often the most competitive rate
  on the board. Great for military families relocating to Maine (Brunswick,
  Bangor/Loring, Kittery)."

### USDA Rural Development
- **Best for:** Properties in eligible rural areas — which is **most of
  Maine outside Portland/South Portland city limits**.
- **Down payment:** 0%.
- **Pitch to Realtors:** "If the property isn't in a dense urban core,
  check USDA eligibility before assuming the buyer needs a big down
  payment."

### Jumbo
- **Best for:** Loan amounts above conforming limits — common for
  waterfront, Midcoast, and Greater Portland luxury properties.
- **Pitch to Realtors:** "We have jumbo options with competitive rates and
  flexible reserve requirements — useful for second-home and luxury
  listings."

### Down Payment Assistance (incl. MaineHousing programs)
- **Best for:** First-time buyers (and some repeat buyers) needing help
  with down payment/closing costs.
- **Pitch to Realtors:** "Pairing DPA with FHA or MaineHousing First Home
  Loan can get a buyer into a home with little to no money out of pocket —
  perfect for renters ready to buy who think they 'can't afford' it yet."

### Self-Employed / Bank Statement Loans
- **Best for:** Business owners, 1099 contractors, gig workers whose tax
  returns don't reflect true cash flow.
- **Pitch to Referral Partners (CPAs especially):** "If a client's tax
  strategy minimizes taxable income, traditional underwriting can hurt
  them. Bank statement loans qualify off deposits, not tax returns — send
  them to me before they assume they can't qualify."

### Investor Loans / DSCR
- **Best for:** Real estate investors buying rental property — qualifies
  based on the property's rental income (Debt Service Coverage Ratio), not
  personal income.
- **Pitch to Realtors/Investors:** "Great for investors scaling a portfolio
  of Maine rentals/vacation properties without W2 income limits."

## Refinance Programs

### Rate & Term / Cash-Out Refinance (Conventional, Jumbo)
- **Best for:** Homeowners lowering their rate, removing PMI, or tapping
  equity for renovations, debt consolidation, or college costs.

### FHA Streamline Refinance
- **Best for:** Existing FHA borrowers — reduced documentation, often no
  appraisal needed.

### VA Streamline Refinance (IRRRL)
- **Best for:** Existing VA borrowers — Interest Rate Reduction Refinance
  Loan, minimal documentation.

### HELOC (Home Equity Line of Credit)
- **Best for:** Homeowners who want flexible access to equity (renovations,
  buying a second property, business needs) without refinancing their first
  mortgage.

## Maine-Specific Notes

- **Seasonality:** Maine's market heats up March-August. Start partner
  outreach and content campaigns in **January-February** so you're top of
  mind before spring listings hit.
- **Second homes/camps:** Huge category in Maine (Lakes Region, Midcoast,
  Western Mountains). Conventional second-home and jumbo programs are
  highly relevant — build relationships with Realtors who specialize in
  recreational property.
- **Heating systems/well & septic:** Rural Maine properties often need
  extra underwriting attention (oil tanks, wells, septic systems). Being
  the LO who *knows* how to navigate these (vs. one who panics) is a major
  differentiator with rural Realtors and USDA-heavy markets (Aroostook,
  Washington, Piscataquis, Oxford counties).
- **New construction/renovation:** Maine has an aging housing stock —
  renovation/rehab loan knowledge (paired with conventional/FHA) is valuable
  for builders and contractors as referral partners.
