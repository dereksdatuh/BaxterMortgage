# Compliance Checklist for Marketing & Outreach

**This is general guidance, not legal advice.** Always confirm specifics
with Baxter Mortgage's compliance/legal team before launching new marketing
campaigns — requirements (especially state-specific advertising rules and
MLO compensation/RESPA gift rules) can change and vary.

## Every Piece of Marketing Should Include
- [ ] Your name + NMLS# 2810853
- [ ] Baxter Mortgage company name + NMLS# 2752768
- [ ] "Equal Housing Lender" / Equal Housing Opportunity logo or statement
- [ ] If discussing specific terms (rate, APR, payment), include required
  Regulation Z (Truth in Lending Act) trigger-term disclosures — when in
  doubt, **don't quote specific rates/payments in ads**; drive to a 1:1
  conversation instead.

## Email/Newsletter Compliance (CAN-SPAM)
- [ ] Include your physical business address (or Baxter Mortgage's) in
  the footer
- [ ] Include a working unsubscribe link/option
- [ ] Honor opt-outs within 10 business days
- [ ] Subject lines must not be misleading

## Texting Compliance (TCPA)
- [ ] Only text people who've given consent (provided their number on an
  application, signed up for updates, or you have an existing business
  relationship)
- [ ] Include opt-out instructions periodically ("Reply STOP to opt out")
- [ ] Don't use auto-dialers/mass texting platforms without proper consent
  capture — check with compliance before using any bulk SMS tool

## Social Media
- [ ] No specific rate/APR claims without full disclosures
- [ ] No guarantees of approval, savings, or specific outcomes ("Guaranteed
  approval!" = red flag to regulators)
- [ ] Client testimonials: get written permission, and be aware some states
  restrict testimonials for MLOs — confirm with compliance
- [ ] Fair Housing: avoid any language that could be seen as steering based
  on race, religion, familial status, disability, etc. — keep messaging
  about loan products and process, not "who belongs" in a neighborhood

## Referral Partner Relationships (RESPA — Section 8)
- [ ] **No payment, gifts of meaningful value, or anything of value in
  exchange for referrals.** This includes cash, gift cards above nominal
  value, and disproportionate marketing co-op arrangements.
- [ ] Lunch-and-learns, educational content, and normal promotional items
  (pens, small-value items) are generally fine, but **always confirm
  current thresholds and rules with compliance** — RESPA enforcement is
  serious and this is the area where LOs most often get into trouble.
- [ ] Co-marketing agreements (e.g., splitting costs of a joint mailer with
  a Realtor) must be carefully structured (Marketing Services Agreements)
  — do not set these up without compliance/legal review.
- [ ] "Desk rental" or office space arrangements with referral sources also
  require compliance review.

## Before Sending Any New Template From This Toolkit
1. Personalize placeholders (names, towns, amounts)
2. Add required disclosures (see `branding/brand-guide.md`)
3. If it mentions specific rates, terms, or guarantees — review with
  compliance first
4. If it involves anything of value going to a referral partner — review
  with compliance first
