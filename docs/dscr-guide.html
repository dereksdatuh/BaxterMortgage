How DSCR Loans Let You Qualify Using Rental Income, Not Yours

What Is a DSCR Loan?

DSCR stands for Debt Service Coverage Ratio. Instead of proving your personal income with tax returns, pay stubs, and W-2s, a DSCR loan looks at whether the property's rental income covers the mortgage payment. If the property pays for itself, you could qualify.

This is designed for: real estate investors (new or experienced), landlords buying their next rental, Airbnb and VRBO buyers, self-employed investors whose tax returns do not reflect their earning power, and anyone scaling a portfolio (up to 20 financed properties).

How to Calculate Your DSCR Ratio

Gross Monthly Rental Income ÷ Monthly Payment (PITI)
PITI = Principal + Interest + Taxes + Insurance

Below 0.80

Example: A property rents for $2,500/month. The mortgage payment (PITI) is $2,000/month.

$2,500 ÷ $2,000 = 1.25 DSCR — This property qualifies. The rental income covers the payment with room to spare.

For purchases, market rent from the appraisal is typically used. For refinances, the lesser of lease amount or market rent may apply.

Traditional Investment Loan vs. DSCR

Traditional Loan

  • Tax returns required
  • W-2s and pay stubs needed
  • DTI ratio matters
  • Complex for self-employed
  • Limited to 10 financed properties
  • Airbnb income hard to document

DSCR Loan

  • No tax returns
  • No W-2s needed
  • DTI not calculated
  • Self-employed friendly
  • Up to 20 financed properties
  • Airbnb/VRBO income accepted

Eligible Properties

Eligible

  • Single-family homes
  • 2-4 unit properties
  • Condos and townhomes
  • Long-term leased properties
  • Vacant (intended rental)
  • Short-term: Airbnb, VRBO (select programs)

Not Eligible

  • Manufactured homes
  • Agricultural zoning
  • Properties over 20 acres
  • Owner-occupied
