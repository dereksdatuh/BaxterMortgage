# Review & Referral Request Templates

The single highest-leverage habit for growing your pipeline: **ask every
happy client for a review AND a referral, every time.** Most loan officers
only do one (or neither).

---

## Timing
Ask **after closing, once they're settled** — typically 1-2 weeks post-
close (Day 30 touchpoint in the "Just Closed" sequence is ideal).

---

## Google Review Request (Text)

> Hi [First Name]! So glad everything went smoothly with your closing. If
> you have 60 seconds, a quick Google review would mean the world to me —
> it really helps other Mainers find a lender they can trust. Here's the
> link: [Google review link]
>
> Thank you so much either way — congrats again on the new home! 🏡

## Google Review Request (Email)

**Subject:** A small favor (60 seconds)?

> Hi [First Name],
>
> Now that you've had a little time to settle in — how's everything going
> with the home and the mortgage? Hope it's been smooth sailing!
>
> If you were happy with how things went, would you mind leaving a quick
> Google review? It genuinely helps — a lot of my business comes from
> people finding me online, and hearing from someone like you carries a lot
> of weight.
>
> Here's the direct link: [Google review link]
>
> Thanks so much, and congrats again!
> Derek

---

## The Referral Ask (Direct but Warm)

**In person/phone (after a positive comment about the experience):**
> "I really appreciate you saying that — honestly, the way my business
> grows is through people like you telling friends and family about their
> experience. If you think of anyone — whether they're buying, selling, or
> just thinking about refinancing — I'd love an introduction. Even just a
> name and number, I'll take it from there and make you look good."

**Text/Email version:**
> One more thing — if you know anyone (friends, family, coworkers) who are
> thinking about buying a home, selling, or refinancing, I'd really
> appreciate an introduction. I promise to take great care of them, just
> like I did for you. Feel free to just send me their name and number, or
> connect us however's easiest for you.

---

## "Two Names" Technique (advanced)

After a glowing review or thank-you, ask specifically:
> "Can you think of two people who might be in a similar spot to where you
> were a year ago — maybe renting and wondering if they could buy, or
> sitting on equity and not sure what their options are? I'd love to just
> have a no-pressure conversation with them, same as I did with you."

This works better than a generic "know anyone?" because it prompts them to
actually think of names.

---

## Testimonial Collection (for website/social use)

> Hi [First Name], would you be open to sharing a short quote about your
> experience working together? Even 2-3 sentences would be great — I'd love
> to feature it (with your permission) on social media or my website. No
> pressure if you'd rather not, totally understand!

*Always get explicit written permission before publishing a client's name,
photo, address, or loan details anywhere public.*
